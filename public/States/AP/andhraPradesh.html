About

Andhra Pradesh, with its capital in Amaravati, is a southern Indian state nestled in the Eastern Ghats. It boasts a rich cultural heritage with a mix of Hindu, Buddhist, and Islamic influences. Its diverse landscape includes a long coastline, fertile plains, and hilly regions. Agriculture, particularly rice cultivation, is vital to the economy, along with growing industrial sectors like IT and pharmaceuticals. Tourist attractions range from historical sites to beautiful beaches. While facing challenges in development and healthcare, Andhra Pradesh offers a fascinating blend of tradition and modernity for study and exploration.

Cultural Experiences

Attend the Deccan Festival in Hyderabad, Explore the Kuchipudi village, known for the traditional Kuchipudi dance form, Enjoy the Lepakshi Dance and Music Festival

    • Chicken curry

    • Varieties in Breakfast

      Try Pesarattu and Upma in the local breakfast menu

    • Savoury sweets

      Enjoy sweets like Pootharekulu and Ariselu

  • Folk Dances and Music

    • Kuchipudi

      Experience Kuchipudi, a classical dance form

    • Telugu Folk

      Enjoy traditional Telugu folk music and songs

    • Dance forms

      Watch Perini Thandavam, a martial dance form

    • Melodious Musics

      Listen to the melodious strains of classical Carnatic music

  • Traditional Handicrafts

    • Kalamkari hand-painted textiles

      Indulge in the exquisite world of Kalamkari hand-painted textiles, where artistry and tradition seamlessly intertwine.

    • Wooden-Toys

      Explore famous Kondapalli wooden toys and crafts

    • Saree art

      Buy Mangalagiri and Gadwal silk sarees

  • Fairs and Festivals

    • Sankranti

      Sankranti is a major harvest festival celebrated with colorful kite flying and traditional foods.

    • Ugadi

      Ugadi marks the Telugu New Year with special dishes and the Panchangam reading.

    • Bonalu

      Bonalu is a folk festival honoring the goddess Mahakali, celebrated with music and offerings.

    • Pongal

      Pongal is a Tamil festival celebrated in Andhra Pradesh with fervor, thanking the sun god for a bountiful harvest.

    • Deccan Festival

      This festival showcases the rich culture and heritage of Andhra Pradesh through dance, music, and crafts.
