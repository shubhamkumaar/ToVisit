About Tirupati

Tirupati, located in Andhra Pradesh, India, is a renowned pilgrimage destination famous for the Sri Venkateswara Temple, dedicated to Lord Venkateswara. This temple atop Tirumala Hills is a major religious site, drawing millions of devotees. Tirupati is known for its annual Brahmotsavam festival and the tradition of devotees offering their hair. The town is surrounded by scenic Seshachalam Hills, and it also has educational institutions and a rich cultural heritage. With well-connected road, rail, and air links, Tirupati offers a unique blend of spirituality, history, and natural beauty.

Visiting Time

Tirupati's Sri Venkateswara Temple is open daily, with darshan available from early morning to late evening. Special pujas and rituals are conducted at specific times, with options for VIP and quick darshan. Free Divya Darshan is also available, but waiting times can be long. During festivals like Brahmotsavam, schedules may vary, so it's best to check with the temple authorities for the latest timings.

History

The Sri Venkateswara Swami Temple is a Hindu temple situated in the hill town of Tirumala at Tirupati in Tirupati district of Andhra Pradesh, India. The Temple is dedicated to Venkateswara, a form of Vishnu, who is believed to have appeared on the earth to save mankind from trials and troubles of Kali Yuga.

Geography

Tirupati is located at13.65°N 79.42°E in the Tirupati district of Andhra Pradesh state in southern India. Tirupati lies at the foot of Seshachalam Hills of Eastern Ghats which were formed during Precambrian era.

Weather

Tirupati, located in the south of India, experiences a tropical climate. Summers (March to June) are hot and dry, with temperatures often exceeding 40°C (104°F). Monsoons (June to September) bring heavy rainfall, leading to cooler temperatures and lush landscapes. Post-monsoon (October to November) offers pleasant weather, while winters (December to February) are mild and comfortable, making it the best time for a visit.

Features

•Sri Venkateswara Temple: The world-famous temple dedicated to Lord Venkateswara is a major pilgrimage center, renowned for its spiritual significance and stunning architecture.

•Natural Beauty: The town is surrounded by the scenic Seshachalam Hills, offering a picturesque backdrop and lush green landscapes.

• Religious Festivals and Culture: Tirupati hosts vibrant religious festivals, showcasing its rich cultural heritage. The town promotes music, dance, and arts as part of its cultural tradition.
