About Kedarnath

Kedarnath Temple is a Hindu Temple, one of the twelve jyotirlinga of shiva

Nestled in the lap of the majestic Himalayas, Kedarnath is a revered pilgrimage site and a paradise for nature lovers. This ancient town in the state of Uttarakhand, India, is not just a destination; it's a spiritual journey. Kedarnath is renowned for the sacred Kedarnath Temple, one of the twelve Jyotirlingas of Lord Shiva. The scenic beauty of the region is unparalleled, with snow-capped peaks, lush green valleys, and gushing rivers creating a mesmerizing atmosphere.

History and Legend

Kedarnath Temple has a rich historical and mythological background. According to legends, the temple was built by the Pandavas, the protagonists of the Indian epic Mahabharata. It is believed that Lord Shiva eluded them repeatedly and finally took refuge here, in the form of a bull, and dived into the ground, leaving behind a hump on the surface. The temple is constructed at this spot where the hump is believed to have appeared.

Architecture

The temple is a fine example of ancient Hindu architecture. It is made of large, heavy, evenly cut grey slabs of stones. The temple's exterior may not be ornate, but its presence amidst the snow-capped peaks of the Himalayas creates a mesmerizing sight. The main shrine houses a sacred conical Shiva Lingam, the symbol of Lord Shiva.

Pilgrimage and Rituals

Every year, thousands of devotees undertake the arduous journey to seek the blessings of Lord Shiva at Kedarnath Temple. The temple opens its doors to devotees from late April to early November, during the summer and autumn months. Pilgrims have to trek a steep 16 km from Gaurikund to reach the temple.

Rituals at Kedarnath

• Abhishekam: The Shiva Lingam is bathed with holy water, milk, ghee, and other sacred substances.

• Aarti: The evening aarti (prayer) is a mesmerizing experience, with priests chanting hymns and the entire temple illuminated with oil lamps.

• Bhog Offerings: Devotees can offer fruits, flowers, and other items to seek the deity's blessings.

Plan Your Pilgrimage

Visiting Kedarnath Temple requires meticulous planning due to its high altitude and challenging terrain. Pilgrims are advised to be physically prepared and carry adequate warm clothing. Accommodation is available in nearby areas, and it's recommended to make advance bookings.
