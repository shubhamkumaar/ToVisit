Discover the Beauty of Uttarakhand

Explore the rich heritage, spirituality, and adventure of Uttarakhand

Amaravati

Heritage Sites

Explore the historical wonders of Uttarakhand, including The Taj Mahal, Fatehpur Sikri, and more.

  • Badrinath Temple : A revered Hindu temple in the Char Dham pilgrimage circuit

  • Kedarnath Temple: Dedicated to Lord Shiva, one of the Char Dhams

  • Rishikesh: Known for its spiritual significance and ancient temples.

Spiritual Journeys

Embark on spiritual adventures to famous Kashi Vishwanath Temple, Ayodhya, and other sacred places.

  • Gangotri: The source of the Ganges River and a Char Dham site

  • Yamunotri: The origin of the Yamuna River, another Char Dham destination

  • Haridwar: Famous for the Ganga Aarti and bathing ghats along the Ganges

Cultural Experiences

Valley of Flowers National Park : A UNESCO World Heritage Site with vibrant alpine flowers

Jim Corbett National Park : India's oldest national park, known for wildlife

Mussoorie: A hill station with colonial architecture and panoramic views

  • Culinary Delights

    • Aloo Ke Gutke

      A popular Garhwali dish made with boiled potatoes

    • Kafuli

      A spinach-based dish, a local delicacy

    • Singori

      A sweet wrapped in Malu leaves, unique to the region

    • Folk Dances and Music

      • Langvir Nritya

        A traditional dance form depicting war and valiant stories

      • Jhora

        A folk dance performed during festivals

      • Garhwali and Kumaoni Music

        Rich in traditional tunes and instruments

    • Traditional Handicrafts

      • Pashmina Shawls

        Known for their warmth and intricate designs

      • Wooden Crafts

        Intricately carved wooden artifacts

      • Ranikhet's Woolen Articles

        Handwoven woolen items

    • Fairs and Festivals

      • Kumbh Mela, Haridwar

        One of the largest spiritual gatherings, it's held every 12 years, attracting millions of pilgrims to bathe in the sacred Ganges.

      • Nanda Devi Raj Jat Yatra

        A 19-day pilgrimage every 12 years, dedicated to Goddess Nanda Devi, it's a unique and arduous trek through the Himalayas.

      • Makar Sankranti, Uttarakhand

        Celebrated with great fervor, it marks the transition of the sun into Capricorn and is famous for the flying of colorful kites.

      • Ganga Dussehra, Rishikesh

        Commemorates the descent of the Ganges from heaven to Earth and is celebrated with ritualistic bathing in the river.

      • Bikhauti Festival

        A prominent festival in the Kumaon region, it's a vibrant celebration of music, dance, and cultural performances.

Experience thrilling activities like river rafting, trekking, and bird watching in Uttarakhand's natural wonders.

    Including treks to Roopkund, Kuari Pass, and more

    • Adventure Level: Moderate

    • Description:"Embark on a journey to the Himalayas, where every step is a story, every peak a poem, and the soul of the mountains whispers tales of adventure and serenity."

  • White Water Rafting in Rishikesh

    Along the Ganges River

    • Location: Badrinath NH-58, Bungee Jumping office, Tapovan Shivpuri, Neelkant Road, Rishikesh, Uttarakhand.

    • Adventure Level: Moderate

    • Description:"Embrace the rush of the Ganges, where every rapid is a story waiting to be told. In Rishikesh, the river whispers adventure and roars with excitement. Dive into the heart-pounding world of white water rafting, and let the waves carry you to unforgettable memories."

  • Skiing in Auli

    A popular winter sports destination

    • Location: Auli Rd, Auli Laga Salude, Uttarakhand 246443.

    • Adventure Level: Easy

    • Description: "As you glide down the pristine slopes of Auli, may your heart race with the thrill of adventure and your soul be soothed by the breathtaking beauty of the Himalayas." Enjoy your time in Auli!
