  • Fairs and Festivals

    • Hornbill Festival

      This is the most famous festival in Nagaland, usually held in December. It's a week-long celebration of Naga culture and heritage, featuring traditional music, dance, and various indigenous games and sports.

    • Moatsu Festival

      Celebrated by the Ao Naga tribe in May, Moatsu is an agricultural festival marked by feasting, singing, dancing, and various rituals.

    • Sekrenyi Festival

      Observed by the Angami tribe in February, Sekrenyi is a purification festival involving traditional cleansing ceremonies and various cultural performances.

    • Aoling Festival

      Celebrated by the Konyak tribe in April, Aoling marks the arrival of spring and involves singing, dancing, and wearing traditional attire.

    • Tuluni Festival

      Observed by the Sumi Naga tribe in July, this festival is a time of feasting and merry-making, accompanied by traditional rituals.
