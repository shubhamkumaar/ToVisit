About Patna

Patna, the capital of Bihar in North India, is a city steeped in cultural heritage, celebrated for its historical significance and traditional arts. It's a hub for commerce, with a notable presence in sectors like agriculture, trade, and education. The city is home to prestigious educational institutions and landmarks such as the Golghar and Patna Museum. While the primary language in Patna is Hindi, English is commonly used, especially in educational and business settings. With accessible healthcare services, a functional airport, and a well-developed transportation network, Patna is a dynamic urban center where the past and present harmoniously coexist.

Visiting Time

Peak Season - The ideal time to visit Patna is from October to February, which covers the post-monsoon period and the entire winter. During these months, the weather is generally pleasant, making it a comfortable and less humid time to explore the city.

History

Patna, formerly known as Pataliputra, is the capital of the state of Bihar and is one of India's oldest cities. It is situated on the southern bank of the Ganges River. With an estimated population of 2.4 million, this ancient city holds a significant place in history and is an important cultural and historical center in India.

Geography

Patna is situated at approximately 25.61°N 85.14°E in the northern part of India, along the southern bank of the Ganges River. The city is nestled in the fertile Gangetic plains. Patna's elevation is relatively low, with an average of about 53 meters, and its highest point reaches around 64 meters above sea level.

Weather

Patna, located in northern India, has a diverse climate with noticeable seasons. Summers, from March to June, are warm and dry, with temperatures frequently rising above 40°C (104°F). The monsoon season, from June to October, brings substantial rainfall and the potential for flooding. The months following the monsoon, November and December, offer more comfortable weather. Winters, in January and February, are mild and present an excellent time for visiting. Patna experiences humidity, especially during the summer and monsoon, but the post-monsoon and winter months are preferred for outdoor activities and tourism.

Features

•Ancient City: Patna, formerly known as Pataliputra, has a rich historical legacy. It was the capital of the Maurya and Gupta empires and an important center of learning during ancient times.

•Historical Landmarks: The city is home to historical landmarks such as the Golghar, a granary built during the British Raj, and Patna Museum, which houses a vast collection of artifacts.

• Arts and Culture: Patna is known for its vibrant cultural scene, with a strong presence of traditional music, dance, and art.
