About

Bihar, capital city is Patna located in eastern India, is a state with a rich historical and cultural heritage. Its capital is Patna, and it boasts a long history, with roots in ancient empires like the Maurya and Gupta. The state's culture is a blend of Hindu, Buddhist, and Islamic influences, and it is known for its festivals and arts. Agriculture is a key part of the economy, with efforts to diversify into industry. Bihar faces challenges like poverty and infrastructure development, but the government is working on improving these areas. The state is famous for tourist destinations like Bodh Gaya and Nalanda and is known for its unique cuisine.

Patna

Patna, the capital of Bihar in North India, is a city steeped in cultural heritage, celebrated for its historical significance and traditional arts.

Cultural Experiences

Indulge in the vibrant Bihari cuisine, folk dances, music, and traditional handicrafts.

  • Culinary Delights

    • Litti Chokha

      Taste the traditional Bihari dish made of wheat flour balls stuffed with roasted gram flour, and served with mashed vegetables

    • Sattu

      Enjoy Sattu, a nutritious flour made from roasted gram or pulses, used in various Bihari delicacies and drinks.

    • Thekua

      Indulge in the sweet and crispy Thekua, a popular Bihari sweet made during festivals

  • Folk Dances and Music

    • Jat-Jatin

      Witness the energetic Jat-Jatin dance, performed during festivals and celebrations, portraying the struggles of farmers.

    • Bidesia

      Enjoy the melodious Bidesia folk songs that depict themes of love, separation, and longing.

    • Sohar-Khilouna

      Experience the traditional folk songs sung during childbirth ceremonies, capturing the essence of family and community bonds

  • Traditional Handicrafts

    • Madhubani Painting

      Explore the intricate and colorful Madhubani paintings, a traditional art form depicting mythological and natural themes.

    • Sujini Embroidery

      Admire the fine Sujini embroidery, a craft form that involves colorful stitching depicting rural life and religious motifs.

    • Bamboo and Tikuli Art

      Witness skilled artisans creating beautiful bamboo crafts and Tikuli art, where paint is applied on glass and later adorned with fine details.

  • Fairs and Festivals

    • Chhath Puja

      Participate in the grand Chhath Puja celebrations, dedicated to the Sun God, where devotees offer prayers and offerings to the rising and setting sun.

    • Sonepur Cattle Fair

      Visit Asia’s largest cattle fair in Sonepur, where traders from across the country gather to buy and sell various animals.

    • Rajgir Mahotsav

      Attend the Rajgir Mahotsav, a cultural festival showcasing classical and folk dance performances, art exhibitions, and craft displays.

Adventure and Nature

Experience thrilling activities like river rafting, trekking, and bird watching in Bihar's natural wonders.

  • River Rafting in Koshi River

    • Location: Koshi River, Saharsa District

    • Adventure Level: Moderate to Challenging

    • Description: Experience the thrill of river rafting in the mighty Koshi River. Navigate through the river's rapids and enjoy the scenic beauty surrounding the area.

  • Valmiki National Park

    • Location: West Champaran District

    • Adventure Level: Easy to Moderate

    • Description: Explore Valmiki National Park, one of the most biodiverse national parks in India. Go on a safari to spot Bengal tigers, leopards, elephants, and various bird species. The park also houses the famous Valmiki Ashram.

  • Trekking in Rajgir Hills

    • Location: Rajgir, Nalanda District

    • Adventure Level: Moderate

    • Description: Embark on a trekking adventure in the picturesque hills of Rajgir. The trails offer stunning views, and you can visit sites like Griddhakuta Peak and Vulture's Peak during your trek

  • Bike Touring in Vikramshila Gangetic Dolphin Sanctuary

    • Location: Bhagalpur District

    • Adventure Level: Easy

    • Description: Explore the Vikramshila Gangetic Dolphin Sanctuary on a bike. Enjoy the scenic ride along the Ganges River and keep an eye out for the endangered Gangetic dolphins in their natural habitat.
