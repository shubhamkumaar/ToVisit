About

Uttar Pradesh (UP), located in northern India, is the most populous state in the country. It boasts a rich historical and cultural heritage, with iconic sites like the Taj Mahal in Agra and the spiritual city of Varanasi. Agriculture is a major part of its economy, and it has a diverse cultural heritage influenced by Hindu and Muslim traditions. UP faces challenges like poverty and infrastructure development, but it is also home to prominent educational institutions. Its capital is Lucknow, and it has a parliamentary system of government, led by a Chief Minister and a Governor appointed by the President of India.

Heritage Sites

Explore the historical wonders of Uttar Pradesh, including The Taj Mahal, Fatehpur Sikri, and more.

  • Taj Mahal : A UNESCO World Heritage Site and one of the Seven Wonders of the World

  • Fatehpur Sikri: A historic city with well-preserved Mughal architecture

  • Varanasi: The spiritual city on the banks of the Ganges with ancient temples and ghats.

Spiritual Journeys

Embark on spiritual adventures to famous Kashi Vishwanath Temple, Ayodhya, and other sacred places.

  • Kashi Vishwanath Temple: One of the holiest Hindu temples in Varanasi

  • Ayodhya: The birthplace of Lord Rama, with Ram Janmabhoomi and Hanuman Garhi

  • Mathura and Vrindavan: Associated with the life of Lord Krishna.

Cultural Experiences

Lucknow : Known for its cultural heritage, Nawabi architecture, and cuisine

Allahabad: A confluence of three sacred rivers and the site of Kumbh Mela

Agra Fort: A UNESCO World Heritage Site showcasing Mughal art and culture

    • Fairs and Festivals

      • Kumbh Mela

        One of the world's largest religious gatherings where millions of devotees take a holy dip in the Ganges to cleanse their sins.

      • Diwali in Ayodhya

        The birthplace of Lord Rama, Ayodhya is beautifully illuminated during Diwali, celebrating the return of Lord Rama.

      • Holi in Mathura

        Mathura, the birthplace of Lord Krishna, hosts a vibrant and colorful celebration of Holi, the festival of colors.

      • Eid-ul-Fitr

        A significant Muslim festival celebrated with prayers, feasting, and the exchange of gifts.

      • Magh Mela

        A month-long fair held at Prayagraj, where devotees take holy dips in the Ganges, especially during Makar Sankranti.
