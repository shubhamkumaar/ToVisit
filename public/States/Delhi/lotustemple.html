Lotus Temple

The Lotus Temple in Delhi, India, is a Bahá'í House of Worship known for its lotus-inspired design. Completed in 1986, its 27 marble petals symbolize unity. Open to all faiths, it offers a serene space for meditation and reflection in lush surroundings, making it a significant cultural and architectural landmark.

History

  1. Design and Architecture: The Lotus Temple is known for its unique lotus flower-inspired design. It was designed by Iranian architect Fariborz Sahba and completed in 1986. The lotus shape is significant in many cultures and symbolizes purity and divinity.
  2. Bahá'í Faith: The Lotus Temple is one of the Bahá'í Houses of Worship. The Bahá'í Faith is a monotheistic religion that emphasizes the unity of all people and religions. The Lotus Temple serves as a place of worship for Bahá'ís and is open to people of all faiths.
  3. Message of Unity: The temple's purpose is to promote the unity of all religions and the oneness of humanity. It welcomes people from all backgrounds to come and pray, meditate, or simply find solace in its serene surroundings.
  4. Awards: The Lotus Temple has won several architectural awards for its innovative and eco-friendly design. It's renowned for its use of natural light and its environmentally conscious construction.
  5. Visitors: The Lotus Temple has become a major tourist attraction in Delhi. It's admired for its architecture and the sense of peace it offers to visitors.
