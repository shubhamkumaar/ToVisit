India Gate

The India Gate is a prominent war memorial in New Delhi, India, built to honor soldiers who died in World War I. Completed in 1931, the arch-shaped monument stands as a symbol of national pride and sacrifice. Surrounded by expansive lawns, it serves as a popular gathering place for locals and tourists. The Amar Jawan Jyoti, an eternal flame, was added later to commemorate India's Unknown Soldier.

Festivals

Wangla

Wangala Festival of Meghalaya is a most popular festival among the Garos of Meghalaya, India. Wangala Festival is a harvest festival held in honour of Saljong, the Sun-god of fertility. The celebration of the Wangala Festival marks the end of a period of toil, which brings good output of the fields.
