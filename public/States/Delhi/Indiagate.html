India Gate

The India Gate is a prominent war memorial in New Delhi, India, built to honor soldiers who died in World War I. Completed in 1931, the arch-shaped monument stands as a symbol of national pride and sacrifice. Surrounded by expansive lawns, it serves as a popular gathering place for locals and tourists. The Amar Jawan Jyoti, an eternal flame, was added later to commemorate India's Unknown Soldier.

Road Access

  1. You can reach India Gate via Rajpath, which is a major avenue in New Delhi.
  2. Rajpath connects Connaught Place (a popular commercial and shopping district) to India Gate.
  3. The distance from Connaught Place to India Gate is approximately 1.5 to 2 kilometers.
  4. The time it takes to travel this distance can vary depending on traffic conditions, but it's usually a short drive or a walkable distance, taking about 10-20 minutes.

History

  1. Construction: India Gate, originally called the All India War Memorial, was built to commemorate the Indian soldiers who died in World War I. Its construction began in 1921 and was completed in 1931.
  2. Architect: The monument was designed by Sir Edwin Lutyens, a prominent British architect.
  3. Architecture: India Gate is a prominent example of the triumphal arch architectural style. It stands at a height of 42 meters and is made of sandstone.
  4. Inscription: The India Gate bears the names of around 13,516 British and Indian soldiers who died during World War I.
