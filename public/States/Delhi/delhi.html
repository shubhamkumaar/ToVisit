About

Delhi, the capital city of India, is a bustling metropolis with a rich historical and cultural tapestry. It is not only the heart of the nation but also a city with a vibrant past. Delhi's history can be traced back through numerous dynasties, including the Mughals, the Delhi Sultanate, and the British colonial period, which have all left their marks on the city. The culture of Delhi is a diverse amalgamation of various influences, reflecting a blend of traditions from across the country. It is a city known for its festivals, arts, and a deep appreciation for history. Delhi's cuisine is equally diverse, offering a taste of the many regional flavors of India.

Cultural Experiences

Delhi offers a rich tapestry of cultural experiences : Dilli Haat, Street Food, Indian Classical Music and Dance, National Handicrafts Museum etc...

  • Culinary Delights

    • Chole Bhature

      A classic North Indian dish of spicy chickpeas served with deep-fried bread. You can find delicious Chole Bhature at places like Sita Ram Diwan Chand and Gali Paranthe Wali.

    • Street Food in Chandni Chowk

      This bustling market in Old Delhi offers a variety of street food, including parathas, kebabs, jalebis, and more. Be sure to visit Karim's for Mughlai cuisine.

    • Dilli Ki Chaat

      Delhi is famous for its chaat, which includes dishes like golgappa (pani puri), aloo tikki, and papri chaat. Try these at popular spots like Natraj and UPSC Chaat Wala.

  • Folk Dances and Music

    • Dilli Haat

      Dilli Haat often hosts cultural events and performances, including folk dances and music from different states of India. Check their event schedule for upcoming performances.

    • Sanskriti Kendra

      This cultural center in Delhi regularly organizes folk dance and music performances, showcasing the diverse traditions of India.

    • Folk Performances at Cultural Festivals

      Look out for cultural festivals in Delhi, such as the Surajkund Crafts Mela, which feature traditional folk dances and music from different regions.

  • Traditional Handicrafts

    • Dilli Haat

      This open-air market is a hub for traditional Indian handicrafts. You can find textiles, pottery, jewelry, and more from various states of India. It's a one-stop shop for a wide variety of crafts.

    • Central Cottage Industries Emporium

      Located on Janpath, this emporium is known for its collection of high-quality handicrafts and handwoven textiles. It's a government-run store and has a wide range of items.

    • Crafts Museum

      The National Handicrafts and Handlooms Museum in Pragati Maidan is an excellent place to explore traditional crafts from different regions of India.

  • Fairs and Festivals

    • Diwali

      The Festival of Lights is one of the most significant festivals in Delhi. The city is beautifully illuminated, and people celebrate with fireworks, traditional sweets, and the lighting of oil lamps (diyas).

    • Holi

      The festival of colors is celebrated with enthusiasm in Delhi. People play with colored powders and water, making it a vibrant and joyous occasion.

    • Durga Puja

      The Bengali community in Delhi celebrates Durga Puja with grand processions, cultural performances, and elaborate idol displays.

Adventure and Nature

Experience thrilling activities like river rafting, trekking, and bird watching in Delhi's natural wonders.

  1. Sanjay Van

    • Location: New Delhi, Delhi

    • Adventure Level: Moderate to Challenging

    • Description:This is a sprawling forest area in the heart of the city, offering a serene escape from the urban hustle and bustle. You can explore its trails and enjoy nature walks.

  2. Asola Wildlife Sanctuary

    • Location: F7V6+53J, Shooting Range Rd, Asola Wild Life Sanctuary, Tughlakabad, New Delhi, Delhi 110044

    • Adventure Level: Easy to Moderate

    • Description: Located in the southern part of Delhi, this sanctuary is home to a variety of flora and fauna. You can explore the trails and birdwatch in this natural oasis.

  3. Okhla Bird Sanctuary

    • Location: Noida Plaza, N Block, Pocket K, Sector 95, Noida, Uttar Pradesh 201301

    • Adventure Level: Moderate

    • Description: This sanctuary along the Yamuna River is a haven for birdwatchers. You can spot a wide range of migratory and resident bird species.

  4. Yamuna River

    • Location: Delhi

    • Adventure Level: Easy

    • Description: While the Yamuna River is not suitable for swimming, it offers opportunities for river walks and picnics along its banks.
