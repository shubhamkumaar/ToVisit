Discover the Beauty of Punjab

Explore the rich heritage, spirituality, and adventure of Punjab

Heritage Sites

Explore the historical wonders of Punjab, including The Golden Temple, Jallianwala Bagh memorial, and more.

  • Golden Temple in Amritsar, a sacred Sikh pilgrimage site

  • Jallianwala Bagh, a memorial for the Jallianwala Bagh massacre

  • Qila Mubarak in Patiala, a historic fort and palace

Spiritual Journeys

Embark on spiritual adventures to most famous Golden Temple, Anandpur Sahib, and other sacred places.

  • Golden Temple, a place of religious significance and architectural beauty

  • Anandpur Sahib, known for the Virasat-e-Khalsa museum and religious events

  • Durgiana Temple in Amritsar, dedicated to Goddess Durga

Cultural Experiences

Bhangra, the energetic and vibrant traditional dance form, Attari-Wagah Border Ceremony, a patriotic and lively event, Visiting the Punjabi Rural Museum for a glimpse of rural life.

  • Culinary Delights

    • Chicken curry

      Butter Chicken, a popular Punjabi dish

    • Varieties in Breakfast

      Sarson da Saag and Makki di Roti, a traditional winter delicacy

    • Famous streetfoods

      Amritsari Kulcha and Chole, a famous street food

  • Folk Dances and Music

    • Bhangra

      Bhangra, a lively dance with upbeat music

    • Giddha dance

      Giddha, a traditional Punjabi dance performed by women

    • Punjabi folk music

      Enjoying live performances of Punjabi folk music

  • Traditional Handicrafts

    • Phulkari embroidery

      Phulkari embroidery, vibrant and colorful handwork

    • Juttis

      Punjabi Juttis, traditional handmade footwear

    • Punjabi attire

      Patiala Salwar, traditional Punjabi attire

  • Fairs and Festivals

    • Lohri

      Lohri is a winter harvest festival celebrated with bonfires, dance, and traditional Punjabi songs.

    • Baisakhi

      Baisakhi marks the Punjabi New Year and the harvest season's onset, celebrated with energetic bhangra dance and special prayers at Gurudwaras.

    • Diwali

      Diwali, the Festival of Lights, is celebrated with lamps, candles, and fireworks, symbolizing the victory of light over darkness.

    • Guru Nanak Jayanti

      This festival celebrates the birth of Guru Nanak, the founder of Sikhism, with religious processions and readings from the Guru Granth Sahib.

    • Hola Mohalla

      Hola Mohalla is a Sikh festival featuring martial arts demonstrations, poetry contests, and other competitive events.
