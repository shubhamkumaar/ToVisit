Visiting Time

Peak Season – The best time to visit Ranchi is typically between the months of October to February. During this period, Ranchi experiences pleasant weather as it transitions from the monsoon season into winter. The temperatures are relatively cooler, making it a comfortable time to explore the city without the discomfort of excessive heat and humidity.

History

Ranchi, the capital of Jharkhand, is located in the eastern part of India. With a population of around 1.5 million, it's a prominent city in the region. Ranchi is known for its natural beauty, surrounded by hills and forests, and is often referred to as the "City of Waterfalls." While it may not be as populous as some of India's larger cities, Ranchi offers a unique blend of urban and natural attractions for visitors to explore.

Geography

Ranchi is situated at approximately 23.36°N latitude and 85.33°E longitude in the northeastern region of India. It's the capital of Jharkhand and is known for its hilly terrain and lush greenery. The city has an average elevation of about 651 meters, with some of its highest points reaching up to 810 meters above sea level. This elevation gives Ranchi a relatively cooler climate compared to many other Indian cities.

Weather

Ranchi, located in the eastern part of India, experiences a subtropical climate with distinctive seasons. Summers (March to June) can be warm, with temperatures ranging from 20°C to 35°C (68°F to 95°F). Monsoons (June to September) bring moderate to heavy rainfall, which is essential for the region's lush green landscapes. Post-monsoon months of October and November offer comfortable and pleasant weather, making it a good time for outdoor activities and exploration. Winters (December to February) are cool and crisp, with temperatures typically falling between 6°C to 22°C (43°F to 72°F). This season is ideal for tourism in Ranchi due to the mild weather and clear skies.

Features

•Natural Beauty: Ranchi is renowned for its natural beauty. The city is surrounded by dense forests, rolling hills, and numerous waterfalls. The mesmerizing Hundru Falls, Jonha Falls, and Dassam Falls are some of the captivating attractions that offer a refreshing escape from the bustling city life.

•Climate: The city experiences a subtropical climate with distinct seasons. Summers are warm, monsoons bring moderate to heavy rainfall, and winters are cool and pleasant.

• Historical Significance: Ranchi has historical importance as it was a hub for the freedom struggle during the British colonial period. The city boasts several historical sites, including the Ranchi Hill, which is associated with Mahatma Gandhi's visit to the city.
