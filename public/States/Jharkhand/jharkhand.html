Discover the Beauty of Jharkhand

Explore the rich heritage, spirituality, and adventure of Jharkhand

Amaravati

Heritage Sites

Explore the historical wonders of Jharkhand, including The Hundru Falls, Baidyanath Jyotirlinga Temple, and more.

  • Hundru Falls : One of the highest waterfalls in Jharkhand

  • Baidyanath Jyotirlinga Temple: A revered Hindu pilgrimage site in Deoghar

  • Jagannath Temple, Ranchi: A modern architectural marvel.

Spiritual Journeys

Embark on spiritual adventures to most famous Amarnath Cave Temple, and other sacred places.

  • Parasnath Hill: A significant pilgrimage site for Jains

  • Rajrappa Temple: Dedicated to the Goddess Chhinnamasta

  • Angrabadi Temple Complex: A cluster of ancient temples in Khunti.

Cultural Experiences

Tribal Culture : Explore the diverse cultures of Santhal, Munda, Oraon, and other tribes

Sarhul Festiva: Celebrating the bond between nature and tribal communities

Chhau Dance: A traditional tribal dance form depicting martial arts.

  • Culinary Delights

    • Thekua:

      A traditional sweet made with wheat flour and jaggery

    • Litti Chokha

      A popular savory dish made with wheat flour and roasted eggplant

    • Rugra

      A traditional dish made with baby brinjals and spices

    • Folk Dances and Music

      • Jhumair

        A vibrant dance form performed during festivals

      • Paika Dance

        Depicting the martial arts of the tribal communities

      • Nagpuri Songs

        Traditional folk songs of Jharkhand

    • Traditional Handicrafts

      • Tussar Silk

        Known for its rich texture and natural golden color

      • Bamboo and Woodcraft

        Intricate crafts made by tribal communities

      • Paitkar Paintings

        Traditional tribal paintings depicting daily life

    • Fairs and Festivals

      • Sarhul

        Sarhul is a tribal festival celebrating the sowing of seeds. It involves worshipping the Sal tree and is marked by traditional dances and rituals.

      • Karma

        Karma is a major tribal festival, dedicated to the worship of trees. It involves singing, dancing, and offerings to nature.

      • Tusu Parab

        Tusu Parab is a harvest festival celebrated by women. They sing traditional Tusu songs and make offerings to the deity.

      • Sohrai

        Sohrai is an important cattle festival where cattle are worshipped and decorated.
