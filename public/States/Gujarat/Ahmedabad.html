About Ahmedabad

Ahmedabad, the largest city in Gujarat, is a vibrant metropolis known for its unique blend of tradition and modernity. It's renowned for its rich cultural heritage, with historical landmarks such as the Adalaj Stepwell and the Sidi Saiyyed Mosque. Ahmedabad is also a significant economic hub, with a strong presence in sectors like textiles, manufacturing, and information technology. The city is home to prestigious educational institutions like the Indian Institute of Management Ahmedabad (IIMA) and boasts a thriving business environment.

History

Ahmedabad, formerly known as Amdavad, is the largest city in the state of Gujarat and one of India's major urban centers. It is located on the banks of the Sabarmati River in western India. With an estimated population of over 7 million, Ahmedabad is among the most populous cities in India and a significant economic and cultural hub in the country.
