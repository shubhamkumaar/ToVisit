Discover the Beauty of Meghalaya

Explore the rich heritage, spirituality, and adventure of Meghalaya

About

Meghalya is a captivating state in northeastern India, known for its lush green landscapes and picturesque beauty. With its rolling hills, misty mountains, and stunning waterfalls, it's often referred to as the "Abode of Clouds." Meghalaya is also renowned for its unique living root bridges, crafted by the local Khasi and Jaintia tribes. This state offers a rich cultural tapestry, with various indigenous communities and their vibrant traditions. Whether you're exploring the charming capital, Shillong, or venturing into the remote villages, Meghalaya is a place of natural wonder and cultural diversity.

Cultural Experiences

  1. Culinary Delights

    • Local Khasi Cuisine

      • Available in local eateries during meal times.

    • Sattu

      Enjoy Sattu, a nutritious flour made from roasted gram or pulses, used in various Bihari delicacies and drinks.

    • Thekua

      Indulge in the sweet and crispy Thekua, a popular Bihari sweet made during festivals

  2. Folk Dances and Music

    • Cultural Events and Festivals

      • Timings vary, often in the evenings and during special occasions.

  3. Traditional Handicrafts

    • Shillong - Lewduh Market (Bara Bazaar):

      Open during the day for shopping.

    • Tura - Rongrenggiri

      Open during the day for traditional textile shopping.

  4. Fairs and Festivals

    • Nongkrem Dance Festival

      A Khasi festival featuring traditional dances to celebrate their culture and heritage.

    • Wangala Festival

      Celebrated by the Garo community, it marks the end of the harvest season with dance and music.

    • Shad Suk Mynsiem

      Khasi festival dedicated to giving thanks to the goddess of Shillong through colorful rituals and dances.
