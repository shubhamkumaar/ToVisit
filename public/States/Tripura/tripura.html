Discover the Beauty of Tripura

Explore the rich heritage, spirituality, and adventure of Tripura

Capital : Agartala

Tripura, a northeastern Indian state, boasts a rich cultural heritage with a fusion of Hindu, Buddhist, and Islamic influences. Its diverse landscape includes lush greenery, hilly terrain, and a serene environment. Agriculture, particularly the cultivation of rice, is pivotal to the economy, along with emerging industrial sectors. Tourist attractions in Tripura range from historical sites to natural beauty. While facing challenges in development and healthcare, Tripura offers a fascinating blend of tradition and modernity for study and exploration.

Heritage Sites

Explore the historical wonders of Manipur, including The Shaheed Minar, Ima Keithel, and more.

  • Ujjayanta Palace : A historic royal palace in Agartala

  • Neermahal: The "Water Palace" located in the middle of Rudrasagar Lake

  • Unakoti: Rock-cut sculptures and ancient carvings.

Spiritual Journeys

Embark on spiritual adventures to famous Shree Govindajee Temple, and other sacred places.

  • Tripura Sundari Temple: Dedicated to the goddess Tripura Sundari

  • Jagannath Bari: A historic temple dedicated to Lord Jagannath

  • Kamalasagar Kali Temple: Situated on the banks of the Kamala Sagar Lake.

Garia Dance: A traditional dance form performed during the Garia Puja

      • Hojagiri Dance

        A traditional dance performed by the Reang community

      • Lebang Boomani

        A harvest dance performed by the Tripuri community

      • Handwoven Textiles

      • Cane and Bamboo Crafts

      • Wood Carvings

    • Fairs and Festivals

      • Ker Puja

        Celebrated by the indigenous Tripuri people, this festival honors the bamboo and seeks blessings for a good harvest.

      • Kharchi Puja

        A week-long festival dedicated to the fourteen deities of the Tripuri pantheon, attracting thousands of devotees.
