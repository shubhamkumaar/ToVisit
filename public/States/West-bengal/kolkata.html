Explore the Vibrant City of Kolkata

Discover the rich culture, history, and cuisine of Kolkata

Details

Location: Kolkata, West Bengal, India

Road Access: Kolkata is well-connected by road from various cities in India, and it has a network of national highways and expressways for easy access.

About Kolkata

Kolkata, the capital of West Bengal, is a city known for its rich cultural heritage, historical significance, and artistic excellence. It's a major economic and cultural hub of India, with a diverse population and a love for literature, art, and cuisine. Kolkata has a unique charm, with iconic landmarks like the Victoria Memorial, Howrah Bridge, and Park Street. The city is also famous for its delicious street food and sweets. Kolkata is a city where tradition meets modernity, and it's a must-visit destination for travelers.

Accommodation

Visiting Time

The best time to visit Kolkata is during the winter months, from October to February, when the weather is pleasant and suitable for outdoor exploration.

History

Kolkata has a rich history dating back to the British colonial era. It played a significant role in India's struggle for independence and is home to many historical sites that reflect its past.

Geography

Kolkata is situated in the eastern part of India, near the Bay of Bengal. It is known for its unique geographical features, including the Hooghly River and the Sundarbans mangrove forest.

Weather

Kolkata experiences a tropical wet-and-dry climate with distinct seasons. Summers are hot and humid, while the monsoon season brings heavy rainfall. Winters are mild and pleasant, making it an ideal time to explore the city.

Features

• Cultural Delights: Kolkata is known for its cultural festivals, music, and dance, with Durga Puja being one of the most celebrated events.

• Historical Significance: The city has numerous historical landmarks and monuments that reflect its colonial past.

• Cuisine: Kolkata offers a variety of delectable street food and sweets, including the famous Rosogolla and Mishti Doi.
