About Chennai

Chennai, the capital of Tamil Nadu in South India, is a city deeply entrenched in cultural heritage, renowned for classical arts and music. It's an economic powerhouse with a strong presence in IT, manufacturing, and the automobile industry. The city is an educational hub, home to esteemed institutions, and boasts historical landmarks such as the Kapaleeshwarar Temple and Fort St. George. Chennai's language of choice is Tamil, but English is widely spoken, especially in business and education. With excellent healthcare facilities, an international airport, and a well-connected transport network, Chennai is a thriving metropolis where tradition and modernity coexist.

Visiting Time

Peak Season – The best time to visit Chennai is between the months of October to February, i.e. from the end of monsoon and all of winter. During this time, the weather is relatively pleasant and it's not that hot and sticky.

History

Chennai, formerly known as Madras, is the capital of the state of Tamil Nadu and is India's fifth largest city. It is located on the Coromandel Coast of the Bay of Bengal. With an estimated population of 8.9 million, the 383-year-old city is the 31st largest metropolitan area in the world

Geography

Chennai is located at 13.04°N 80.17°E on the southeast coast of India and in the northeast corner of Tamil Nadu. It is located on a flat coastal plain known as the Eastern Coastal Plains. The city has an average elevation of 6 metres, its highest point being 60 m.

Weather

Chennai, situated in southern India, experiences a tropical climate with distinct seasons. Summers (March to June) are hot and dry, with temperatures often exceeding 40°C (104°F). Monsoons (June to October) bring heavy rainfall and can lead to flooding. Post-monsoon months of November and December offer more pleasant weather. Winters (January and February) are mild, making it an ideal time to visit. Chennai's humidity is notable, particularly during the summer and monsoon, but the post-monsoon and winter months are preferred for outdoor activities and tourism.

Features

•Historical and Cultural Riches: Chennai boasts a rich historical heritage with landmarks like the Charminar and Kapaleeshwarar Temple, and it celebrates diverse cultural traditions, including vibrant music and dance festivals.

•Economic Powerhouse: The city is a major economic hub, with a strong presence in IT, manufacturing, and various industries, contributing significantly to India's economy.

• Culinary Delights and Modern Infrastructure: Chennai offers a diverse culinary experience, including iconic dishes like Hyderabadi Biryani, while its modern infrastructure, including a growing metro system, enhances connectivity and livability.
