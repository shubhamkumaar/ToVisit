History

Guwahati, formerly known as Gauhati, is the largest city in the state of Assam and is located in the northeastern region of India. Situated on the banks of the Brahmaputra River, this city with a rich history and culture is the heart of Assam. With an estimated population of over a million, Guwahati is a significant urban center in the northeastern part of India, contributing to the region's growth and development.

Geography

Guwahati is situated at approximately 26.1445°N 91.7362°E in the northeastern region of India. It is located in the state of Assam, nestled on the southern bank of the Brahmaputra River. The city is part of the Assam Valley and is surrounded by picturesque landscapes. Guwahati's elevation is relatively low, with an average of around 55 meters above sea level, while its highest point reaches about 60 meters.

Weather

Guwahati, located in northeastern India, experiences a subtropical climate with distinct seasons. Summers (March to June) can be quite warm and dry, with temperatures sometimes reaching above 35°C (95°F). The monsoon season (June to September) brings heavy rainfall and occasional flooding in some areas. The post-monsoon months of November and December offer more pleasant and moderate weather. Winters (January and February) are relatively mild, making this period an excellent time for visiting Guwahati. The city does experience notable humidity, especially during the summer and monsoon months, but the post-monsoon and winter seasons are favored for outdoor activities and tourism.

Features

•Tourism: Guwahati is a popular tourist destination due to its natural beauty and cultural attractions. The Kaziranga National Park, famous for its one-horned rhinoceros, is not far from the city.

•Economic Hub: The city serves as the economic hub of Assam and the entire northeastern region. It is a major center for trade, commerce, and industry.
