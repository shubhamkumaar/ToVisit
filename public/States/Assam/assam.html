Discover the Beauty of Assam

Explore the rich heritage, spirituality, and adventure of Assam

Heritage Sites

Explore the historical wonders of Assam, including The Kamakhya Temple, Sivasagar monuments, and more.

  • Kamakhya Temple in Guwahati, a revered Hindu pilgrimage site.

  • Sivasagar, with historic monuments like Rang Ghar and Talatal Ghar.

  • Majuli, the world's largest river island.

Spiritual Journeys

Embark on spiritual adventures to Kaziranga National Park, Umananda Island, and other sacred places.

  • Kaziranga National Park, home to the Indian one-horned rhinoceros.

  • Umananda Island, known for the Umananda Temple.

  • Hajo, a pilgrimage site with the Hayagriva Madhava Temple and Powa Mecca.

Cultural Experiences

Bihu Festival, Assam's most celebrated festival with traditional dance and music, Majuli's vibrant Satras, centers for traditional performing arts, Sualkuchi, the silk village known for Muga silk weaving

  • Culinary Delights

    • Assam-Tea

      Assam Tea, world-renowned for its quality

    • Laksa

      Assam Laksa, a traditional noodle soup with a tangy twist

    • Traditional Varieties

      Poita Bhat and Pitika, traditional Assamese comfort food

  • Folk Dances and Music

    • Bihu Dance

      Bihu Dance, a joyful and energetic dance form

    • Traditional songs

      Zikirs and Borgeets, traditional devotional songs

    • Assam instruments and music

      Enjoying traditional Bihu music with instruments like the dhol and pepa

    • Melodious Musics

      Listen to the melodious strains of classical Carnatic music

  • Traditional Handicrafts

    • Saree art

      Muga Silk and Assam Silk Sarees, known for their quality and intricate designs

    • Jewelry

      Assamese jewelry, including traditional Assamese necklace designs

    • Craft work

      Traditional bamboo and cane craftwork

  • Fairs and Festivals

    • Bihu Festival

      Bihu is the most celebrated Assamese festival, marking the Assamese New Year with traditional dance and music.

    • Ambubachi Mela

      Held at Kamakhya Temple, it's a significant religious fair where devotees gather to celebrate the menstrual cycle of the goddess.

    • Bohag Bihu

      Also known as Rongali Bihu, it celebrates the Assamese New Year with feasting, music, and traditional dances.

    • Magh Bihu

      A harvest festival with feasting, bonfires, and cultural performances.

    • Doul Utsav

      Celebrated with joy and fervor, it involves smearing colored powders and water on each other.
