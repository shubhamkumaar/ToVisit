Agra Fort

Agra Fort, also known as the "Red Fort of Agra," is a mesmerizing architectural masterpiece located in the historic city of Agra, India. Nestled on the banks of the Yamuna River, Agra Fort is a symbol of strength and opulence. It's not just a fortress; it's a living chronicle of India's rich history and a must-visit destination for anyone seeking to immerse themselves in the grandeur of the Mughal Empire.

Location

Agra Fort is located in the city of Agra, in the northern Indian state of Uttar Pradesh. It is situated near the banks of the Yamuna River

Weather

The best time to visit Agra is during the winter months from November to March

  • The summer months in Agra are from March to June and are very humid
  • The coldest month of the year is January, with an average low of 47°F and high of 70°F.
  • The monsoon season is from June to October. This is a welcome relief from the heat.

Highlight

Agra Fort is a UNESCO World Heritage site and a historical fort in Agra, India. It was the main residence of the Mughal Dynasty emperors until 1638. The fort is located about 2.5 km northwest of the Taj Mahal.

  1. Jahangiri Mahal: The largest structure in the fort, this was built by Akbar as a private palace for his son Jahangir.
  2. Musamman Burj: An octagonal tower
  3. Diwan-I-Khas: The hall of private audience
  4. Diwan-I-Aam: The hall of public audience
  5. The Pearl Mosque (Moti Masjid): Built by Shah Jahan

History

Agra Fort, also known as Agra's Red Fort, was built in the 16th century.

  • The fort was commissioned by Mughal emperor Akbar in 1565 and completed in 1573.
  • The fort was built to be the main residence of the Mughals. It took over 4,000 workers eight years to complete the fort.
  • The fort was the main residence of the Mughal emperors until 1638. The capital was then shifted from Agra to Delhi.
  • The fort has been owned by various dynasties, including the Lodhi Dynasty, Mughal Empire, and British Empire.
  • The fort is a UNESCO World Heritage site. It is located about 2.5 km northwest of the Taj Mahal.

Festivals

Taj Mahotsav

A 10-day festival that takes place annually at Shilpgram in Agra, India. The festival is inspired by the Mughal era and nawabi style of the 18th and 19th centuries.


Ram Barat

A popular local festival that celebrates the marriage procession of Sri Ram.


Kailash Fair

A festival that honors Lord Shiva.
